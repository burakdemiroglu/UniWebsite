Faculties

Name Information
Engineering Our faculty originated in 1968 with the establishment of HASTAŞ Private High School Eskişehir Chemical Engineering. By 1970, it evolved into the Eskişehir State Engineering-Architecture Academy. Following the educational restructuring in Eskişehir in 1983, it became part of Anadolu University as the Faculty of Engineering and Architecture. In 1993, a significant portion of the faculty, including staff and resources, transitioned to Eskişehir Osmangazi University by decree. However, the Faculty of Engineering resumed its educational endeavors in the 1994-1995 academic year with three departments, operating from various buildings on Yunus Emre Campus until 1998. Since October 1998, it has operated from its own buildings on İki Eylül Campus. In 2012, the Department of Architecture separated to form the Faculty of Architecture and Design, prompting the name change to "Faculty of Engineering." Currently, the Faculty of Engineering is under the auspices of Eskişehir Technical University, established by Law No. 7141 on May 18, 2018.
Architecture The Faculty of Architecture and Design, which was established by the decision of the Council of Ministers numbered 2011/2471 on 14.11.2011, continues its education and training as Eskişehir Technical University by being affiliated with the rectorate with the Higher Education Law published in the Official Gazette published on 18.05.2018.
Science The Faculty of Science at Eskişehir Technical University aims to be a leading institution in generating fundamental knowledge for scientific innovations and providing comprehensive education to society through various media. It covers disciplines such as Physics, Chemistry, Biology, Mathematics, and Statistics, emphasizing laboratory-based teaching with state-of-the-art technology. The curriculum includes vocational and social elective courses to foster versatile, confident, and knowledgeable individuals prepared for research roles. The faculty prioritizes modern, well-equipped laboratories and classrooms to produce rational, questioning, and research-oriented graduates in biology, physics, statistics, chemistry, and mathematics. A significant portion of courses in the Statistics and Chemistry Departments are taught in English, with compulsory English preparatory classes. Optional English preparatory classes are available for Biology, Physics, and Mathematics Departments, lasting one year. All departments hold FEDEK accreditation for the second consecutive time until September 30, 2023. The Faculty of Science operates under Eskişehir Technical University, established by Law No. 7141 on May 18, 2018.

Institute of Transportation Sciences Transportation Economics Research Institute was established within Anadolu University in 1993 by Decree Law No. 496, affiliated with the Rectorate. Changing the name of the Transport Economics Research Institute to "Transportation Sciences Institute" was decided by the Council of Ministers on 4/10/2012 and came into force by being published in the Official Gazette No. 28455 dated 02 November 2012. The Institute of Transportation Sciences is the first and only institute in its field.
Institute of Earth and Space Sciences The Institute of Earth and Space Sciences started its activities in 1989 with research on Remote Sensing and Geographic Information Systems, affiliated with Anadolu University, Computer Research and Application Center, and its research activities continued within the scope of the Satellite and Space Sciences Research Institute in 1993. With the Official Gazette No. 28455 published on 02 November 2012, its name was changed to the Institute of Earth and Space Sciences as of 04 November 2012.
